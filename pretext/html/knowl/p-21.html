Several of the sets of numbers we've been discussing can be split up based on the so-called trichotomy property: every real number is either positive, negative or zero. In particular, \(\Integers\), \(\Rationals\) and \(\Reals\) can have modifiers stuck on so that we can discuss (for example) the negative real numbers, or the positive rational numbers or the integers that aren't negative. To do this, we put superscripts on the set symbols, either a \(+\) or a \(-\) or the word “noneg.”
