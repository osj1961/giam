Item h

The Fibonacci numbers are a sequence of integers defined by the rule that a number in the sequence is the sum of the two that precede it. \begin{equation*} F_{n+2} = F_n + F_{n+1} \end{equation*} The first two Fibonacci numbers (actually the zeroth and the first) are both 1. Thus, the first several Fibonacci numbers are \begin{equation*} F_0 = 1, F_1=1, F_2=2, F_3=3, F_4=5, F_5=8, F_6=13, F_7=21, \; \mbox{et cetera} \end{equation*} Use mathematical induction to prove the following formula involving Fibonacci numbers. \begin{equation*} \sum_{i=0}^n (F_i)^2 \, = \, F_n \cdot F_{n+1} \end{equation*}
