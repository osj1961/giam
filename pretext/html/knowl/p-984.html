Basis: Notice that when \(n=0\) the sum on the left-hand side has no terms in it! This is known as an empty sum, and by definition, an empty sum's value is \(0\). Also, when \(n=0\) the formula on the right-hand side becomes \((0 \cdot 1)/2\) and this is \(0\) as well. 1 If you'd prefer to avoid the “empty sum” argument, you can choose to use \(n=1\) as the basis case. The theorem should be restated so the universe of discourse is positive naturals.
