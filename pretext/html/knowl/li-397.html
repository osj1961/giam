Item d

The “socks and shoes” rule is a very silly little mnemonic for remembering how to invert a composition. If we think of undoing the process of putting on our socks and shoes (that's socks first, then shoes) we have to first remove our shoes, then take off our socks. The socks and shoes rule is valid for relations as well. Prove that \((\relS \circ \relR)^{-1} = \relR^{-1} \circ \relS^{-1}\).
