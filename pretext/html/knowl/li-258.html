Prove (by contraposition) that for all integers \(x\) and \(y\), if \(x+y\) is odd, then \(x\neq y\). \hint{Well, the problem says to do this by contraposition, so let's write down the contrapositive: \begin{equation*} \forall x, y \in \Integers, \; x=y \, \implies \, x+y \; \mbox{is even} . \end{equation*} But proving that is obvious! }
