Let's start by considering the set \(P = \Reals \times \Reals\) — i.e. \(P\) is the \(x\)–\(y\) plane. There are two functions, whose domain is \(P\) that “pick out” the \(x\), and/or \(y\) coordinate. These functions are called \(\pi_1\) and \(\pi_2\), \(\pi_1\) is the projection onto the first coordinate and \(\pi_2\) is the projection onto the second coordinate. 2 Don't think of the usual \(\pi \approx 3.14159\) when looking at \(\pi_1\) and \(\pi_2\). These functions are named as they are because \(\pi\) is the Greek letter corresponding to `p' which stands for “projection.”
