Item d

A Sophie Germain prime is a prime number \(p\) such that the corresponding odd number \(2p+1\) is also a prime. For example 11 is a Sophie Germain prime since \(23 = 2\cdot 11 + 1\) is also prime. Almost all Sophie Germain primes are congruent to \(5 \pmod{6}\), nevertheless, there are exceptions — so the statement “There are Sophie Germain primes that are not 5 mod 6.” is true. Verify this. \hint{The exceptions are very small prime numbers. You should be able to find them easily.}
