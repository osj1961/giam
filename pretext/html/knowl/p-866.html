In proving a set-theoretic identity, we are basically showing that two sets are equal. One reasonable way to proceed is to show that each is contained in the other. Prove that \(A \cap (B \cup C) = (A \cap B) \cup (A \cap C)\) by showing that \(A \cap (B \cup C) \subseteq (A \cap B) \cup (A \cap C)\) and \((A \cap B) \cup (A \cap C) \subseteq A \cap (B \cup C)\).
