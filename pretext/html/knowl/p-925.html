Russell himself developed a workaround for the paradox which bears his name. Together with Alfred North Whitehead he published a 3 volume work entitled Principia Mathematica 2 Isaac Newton also published a 3 volume work which is often cited by this same title, Philosophiae Naturalis Principia Mathematica. [17]. In the Principia, Whitehead and Russell develop a system known as type theory which sets forth principles for avoiding problems like Russell's paradox. Basically, a set and its elements are of different “types” and so the notion of a set being contained in itself (as an element) is disallowed.
