Inductive step: Suppose that \(k\) is a natural number with \(k > 4\), and that \(2^k \lt k!\). Multiply the left hand side of this inequality by \(2\) and the right hand side by \(k+1\) 2 It might be smoother to justify this step by first proving the lemma that \(\forall a,b,c,d \in {\mathbb R}^+, \; a\lt b \land c\lt d \implies ac \lt bd\). to get \begin{equation*} 2\cdot 2^{k} \lt (k+1) \cdot k!. \end{equation*}
