It is possible to generalize the argument that shows a line segment is equivalent to a line to higher dimensions. In two dimensions we would show that the unit disk (the interior of the unit circle) is equinumerous with the entire plane \(\Reals \times \Reals\). In three dimensions we would show that the unit ball (the interior of the unit sphere) is equinumerous with the entire space \(\Reals^3 = \Reals \times \Reals \times \Reals\). Here we would like you to prove the two-dimensional case. Gnomonic projection is a style of map rendering in which a portion of a sphere is projected onto a plane that is tangent to the sphere. The sphere's center is used as the point to project from.
