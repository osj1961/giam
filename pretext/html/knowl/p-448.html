In the 17th century, Pierre de Fermat made the conjecture 1 Fermat's more famous conjecture, that \(x^n+y^n=z^n\) has no non-trivial integer solutions if \(n\) is an integer with \(n>2\) was discovered after his death. that \(\forall x \in {\mathbb N}, P(x)\). No doubt, this seemed reasonable to Fermat because the numbers given by this formula (they are called Fermat numbers in his honor) are all primes — at first! Fermat numbers are conventionally denoted with a subscripted letter F, \(F_n = 2^{2^n}+1\), the first five Fermat numbers are prime.
