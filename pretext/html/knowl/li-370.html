Item a

Write an inductive proof of the formula for the sum of the first \(n\) cubes. \hint{

Theorem5.2.6
\begin{equation*} \forall n \in \Naturals, \; \sum_{k=1}^n k^3 \;= \; \left( \frac{n(n+1)}{2} \right)^2 \end{equation*}
Proof

(By mathematical induction)

Base case: (\(n=1\)) For the base case, note that when \(n=1\) we have \begin{equation*} \sum_{k=1}^n k^3 \; = \; 1 \end{equation*} and \begin{equation*} \left( \frac{n(n+1)}{2} \right)^2 \; = \; 1. \end{equation*}

Inductive step:

Suppose that \(m>1\) is an integer such that \begin{equation*} \sum_{k=1}^m k^3 \; = \; \left( \frac{m(m+1)}{2} \right)^2 \end{equation*}

Add \((m+1)^3\) to both sides to obtain \begin{equation*} (m+1)^3 + \sum_{k=1}^m k^3 \;= \; \left( \frac{m(m+1)}{2} \right)^2 + (m+1)^3. \end{equation*}

Thus \begin{gather*} \sum_{k=1}^{m+1} k^3 \;= \; \left( \frac{m^2(m+1)^2}{4} \right) + \frac{4(m+1)^3}{4}\\ \;= \; \left( \frac{m^2(m+1)^2 + 4 (m+1)^3}{4} \right)\\ \; = \; \left( \frac{(m+1)^2 (m^2 + 4(m+1))}{4} \right)\\ \; = \; \left( \frac{(m+1)^2 (m^2 + 4m +4)}{4} \right)\\ \; = \; \left( \frac{(m+1)^2 (m+2)^2}{4} \right)\\ \; = \; \left( \frac{(m+1)(m+2)}{2} \right)^2 \end{gather*}
