Item d

Consider the alternating sum of squares: \begin{gather*} 1\\ 1 - 4 = -3\\ 1 - 4 + 9 = 6\\ 1 - 4 + 9 - 16 = -10\\ \mbox{et cetera} \end{gather*} Guess a general formula for \(\sum_{i=1}^n (-1)^{i-1} i^2\), and prove it using PMI. \hint{

Theorem5.2.7
\begin{equation*} \forall n \in \Naturals, \; \sum_{i=1}^n (-1)^{i-1} i^2 \;= \; (-1)^{n-1} \frac{n(n+1)}{2} \end{equation*}
Proof

(By mathematical induction)

Base case: (\(n=1\)) For the base case, note that when \(n=1\) we have \begin{equation*} \sum_{i=1}^n (-1)^{i-1} i^2 \;= \; 1 \end{equation*} and also \begin{equation*} (-1)^{n-1} \frac{n(n+1)}{2} \;= \; 1. \end{equation*}

Inductive step:

Suppose that \(k>1\) is an integer such that \begin{equation*} \sum_{i=1}^k (-1)^{i-1} i^2 \;= \; (-1)^{k-1} \frac{k(k+1)}{2}. \end{equation*}

Adding \((-1)^{k} (k+1)^2\) to both sides gives \begin{gather*} \sum_{i=1}^{k+1} (-1)^{i-1} i^2 \;= \; (-1)^{k-1} \frac{k(k+1)}{2} + (-1)^{k} (k+1)^2\\ \;= \; (-1)^{k-1} \frac{k(k+1)}{2} - (-1)^{k-1} (k+1)^2\\ \;= \; (-1)^{k-1} \left( \frac{k(k+1)}{2} - \frac{2(k+1)^2}{2} \right)\\ \;= \; (-1)^{k} \left( \frac{2(k+1)^2}{2} - \frac{k(k+1)}{2} \right)\\ \;= \; (-1)^{k} \frac{(k+1)(2(k+1)-k)}{2}\\ \;= \; (-1)^{k} \frac{(k+1)(k+2)}{2} \end{gather*}
