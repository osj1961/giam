Mark off the beginning and the end of your proofs as a hint to your readers. In this book we start off a proof by writing Proof: in italics and we end every proof with the abbreviation Q.E.D. 1 Quod erat demonstrandum or “(that) which was to be demonstrated.” some authors prefer placing a small rectangle at the end of their proofs, but Q.E.D. seems more pompous.
