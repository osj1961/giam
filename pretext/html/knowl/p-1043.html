A slight restatement of Fermat's little theorem is that \(p\) is always a divisor of \(x^p-x\) (assuming \(p\) is a prime and \(x\) is an integer). Math professors enjoy using their knowledge of Fermat's little theorem to cook up divisibility results that can be proved using mathematical induction. For example, consider the following: \begin{equation*} \forall n \in \Naturals, 3 \divides (n^3 + 2n + 6). \end{equation*}
