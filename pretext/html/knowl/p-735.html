Recall the Euclidean algorithm that was used to calculate the greatest common divisor of two integers \(a\) and \(b\) (which we denote \(\gcd{a}{b}\)). There is a rather important question concerning algorithms known as the “halting problem.” Does the program eventually halt, or does it get stuck in an infinite loop? We know that the Euclidean algorithm halts (and outputs the correct result) because we know the following unique existence result. \begin{equation*} \forall a, b \in \Integers^+, \, \exists ! \, d \in \Integers^+ \; \mbox{such that} \, d=\gcd{a}{b} \end{equation*}
