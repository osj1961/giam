Prove the following formula for a product. \begin{equation*} \prod_{i=2}^n \left(1 - \frac{1}{i}\right) = \frac{1}{n} \end{equation*} \hint{ Notice that the problem statement didn't specify the domain â€” but the smallest value of \(n\) that gives a non-empty product on the left-hand side is \(n=2\).
Proof

(By mathematical induction)

Base case: (\(n=2\)) For the base case, note that when \(n=2\) we have \begin{equation*} \prod_{i=2}^2 \left(1 - \frac{1}{i}\right) = \left(1 - \frac{1}{2}\right) = 1/2 \end{equation*} and, when \(n=2\), the right-hand side (\(1/n\)) also evaluates to \(1/2\).

Inductive step:

Suppose that \(k>2\) is an integer such that \begin{equation*} \prod_{i=2}^k \left(1 - \frac{1}{i}\right) = \frac{1}{k}. \end{equation*}

Then, \begin{gather*} \prod_{i=2}^{k+1} \left(1 - \frac{1}{i}\right)\\ = \left(1 - \frac{1}{k+1}\right) \; \cdot \; \prod_{i=2}^{k} \left(1 - \frac{1}{i}\right)\\ = \left(1 - \frac{1}{k+1}\right) \; \cdot \; \frac{1}{k}\\ = \frac{1}{k+1}. \end{gather*}
