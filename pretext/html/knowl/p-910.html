Use Venn diagrams to show that the following set equivalence is false. \begin{equation*} (A \cup B) \cap (C \cup D) \; = \; (A \cup C) \cap (B \cup D) \end{equation*} \hint{After constructing Venn diagrams for both sets you should be able to see that there are \(4\) regions where they differ. One is \(A \cap B \cap \overline{C} \cap \overline{D}\). What are the other three?}
