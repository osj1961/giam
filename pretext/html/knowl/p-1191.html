Let \(x\), \(y\) and \(z\) be arbitrary natural numbers, and further suppose that both \(x \relS y\) and \(y \relS z\). From the definition of \(\relS\) we deduce that \(sf(x) = sf(y)\) and \(sf(y) = sf(z)\). Clearly, \(sf(x) = sf(z)\) (this deduction comes from the transitive property of \(=\)), so \(x \relS z\).
