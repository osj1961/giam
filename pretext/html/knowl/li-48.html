Item b

Dividing an integer by two has an interesting interpretation when using binary notation: simply shift the digits to the right. Thus, \(22 = 10110_2\) when divided by two gives \(1011_2\) which is \(8+2+1=11\). How can you recognize a doubly-even integer from its binary representation? \hint{Even numbers have a zero in their units place. What digit must also be zero in a doubly-even number's binary representation?}
