If-then control statements are decision makers. They first calculate a Boolean expression (this is just a fancy way of saying something that is either true or false), and send program flow to different locations depending on that result. A small example will serve as an illustration. Suppose that in the body of an algorithm we wish to check if 2 variables, \(x\) and \(y\) are equal, and if they are, increment \(x\) by 1.
