Rene Descartes, the famous mathematician and philosopher, was the first to consider coordinatizing the plane and thus is responsible for our current understanding of the relationship between geometry and algebra. Rene Descartes' name is also memorialized in the definition of the Cartesian product of sets, and the plane is nothing more than the product \(\Reals \times \Reals\). Indeed, the plane provided the very first example of the concept that was later generalized to the Cartesian product of sets.
