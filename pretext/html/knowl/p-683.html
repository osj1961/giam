True or false: Whenever an integer \(n\) is a divisor of the square of an integer, \(m^2\), it follows that \(n\) is a divisor of \(m\) as well. (In symbols, \(\forall n \in \Integers, \forall m \in \Integers, n \mid m^2 \; \implies \; n \mid m\).) Prove your answer. \hint{Hint: List all of the divisors of \(36 = (2\cdot 3)^2\). See if any of them are bigger than \(6\).}
