The main idea is simply to move to 3-dimensional space. Imagine the original flat plane containing our three random circles as being the plane \(z=0\) in Euclidean 3-space. Replace the three circles by three spheres of the same radius and having the same centers — clearly the intersections of these spheres with the plane \(z=0\) will be our original circles. While pairs of circles are encompassed by two lines (the external tangents that we've been discussing so much), when we have a pair of spheres in 3-space, they are encompassed by a cone which lies tangent to both spheres 2 As before, when the spheres happen to have identical radii we get a degenerate case — the cone becomes a cylinder.. Notice that the cones that lie tangent to a pair of spheres intersect the plane precisely in those infamous external tangents.
