The lexicographic order, \(\lt _{\mbox{lex} }\), is a relation on the set of all words, where \(x \lt _{\mbox{lex} } y\) means that \(x\) would come before \(y\) in the dictionary. Consider just the three letter words like “iff”, “fig”, “the”, et cetera. Come up with a usable definition for \(x_1x_2x_3 \lt _{\mbox{lex} } y_1y_2y_3\).
