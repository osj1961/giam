Item c

The sum of the first \(n\) natural numbers is sometimes called the \(n\)-th triangular number \(T_n\). Triangular numbers are so-named because one can represent them with triangular shaped arrangements of dots. \ifx\SetFigFont\undefined\gdef\SetFigFont#1#2#3#4#5{ \reset@font\fontsize{#1}{#2pt} \fontfamily{#3}\fontseries{#4}\fontshape{#5} \selectfont}\fi The first several triangular numbers are 1, 3, 6, 10, 15, et cetera. Determine a formula for the sum of the first \(n\) triangular numbers \(\displaystyle \left( \sum_{i=1}^n T_i \right)\) and prove it using PMI. \hint{The formula is \(\frac{n(n+1)(n+2)}{6}\).}
